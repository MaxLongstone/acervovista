2. What AcervoVista Is and Is Not

2.1 What AcervoVista Is

AcervoVista is a document intelligence and estate organization platform. It provides:

  • An intake process that builds a skeleton case file from information you provide
  • Document upload, OCR text extraction, and document classification
  • A timeline reconstruction of dated events based on uploaded documents
  • A gap map identifying which documents are present, pending, or missing for a given estate type and jurisdiction
  • Plain language summaries of what uploaded documents appear to contain
  • An AI-powered assistant that answers questions about documents, process steps, and estate administration generally
  • A Lawyer Handoff Package — a structured PDF export of your case file organized for a lawyer's review
  • Referrals to licensed attorneys in your jurisdiction

2.2 What AcervoVista Is Not

Critical Limitation: AcervoVista is not a law firm. It does not provide legal advice, legal representation, or legal strategy. Nothing produced by AcervoVista constitutes legal advice or should be relied upon as such.

Specifically, AcervoVista does not:

  • Advise you on your legal rights or entitlements under any will, trust, or succession law
  • Tell you whether any transfer, disposition, or action was lawful or unlawful
  • Represent you in any legal proceeding or negotiation
  • Guarantee the accuracy, completeness, or legal sufficiency of any document it processes or any output it produces
  • Substitute for the advice of a licensed attorney, which we strongly recommend for any estate matter of meaningful complexity

4. No Legal Advice — Scope and Limits of the Platform

4.1 Chronology, Not Judgment

AcervoVista's consumer tier describes what documents show and when events happened. It does not characterize any event, transfer, disposition, or action as suspicious, fraudulent, improper, unlawful, or a cause for legal concern. Those characterizations require legal judgment and are the province of a licensed attorney, not this platform.

4.2 The AI Assistant

AcervoVista includes an AI-powered assistant powered by Anthropic's Claude API. The assistant is designed to answer questions about documents, explain what process steps mean generally, and help users understand their case file. The assistant is explicitly programmed not to provide legal conclusions, legal strategy, or characterizations of conduct.

The AI assistant is limited to 50 turns per case (the "Turn Cap"). This limit exists to manage costs and to encourage users to seek legal advice for complex questions. The Turn Cap may be increased by contacting support.

Responses from the AI assistant are generated by artificial intelligence and may contain errors, omissions, or inaccuracies. Do not rely on assistant responses as a substitute for legal advice.

Confirm that the scope of the AI assistant — answering questions about documents and process, refusing legal conclusions, redirecting to lawyers — is structured to avoid unauthorized practice of law (UPL) in all jurisdictions where the platform operates. Florida Bar Rule 4-5.5 and equivalent rules in other jurisdictions apply.

4.3 The Lawyer Handoff Package

The Lawyer Handoff Package is a structured PDF export of your case file organized for a lawyer's first-day review. It is a document organization tool, not a legal brief or legal opinion. It does not constitute legal advice. The package carries a disclaimer on every page to this effect.

4.4 Gap Map and Document Classification

The gap map identifies documents typically required for estate administration in a given jurisdiction and case type. It does not constitute legal advice about what documents are required in your specific case. Document classification is performed by OCR and AI analysis. Classifications may be inaccurate. You are responsible for reviewing and correcting document classifications where they are wrong.
