How it works

Three things.
In that order.

No legal jargon. No assumptions. The picture of what you hold — built from what you know and what you upload.

01 — Tell us what you know

The intake

Answer questions anyone can answer from memory. Who passed. When. What you know about the assets. Who the heirs are. The system builds a skeleton case file before you upload a single document.

02 — Photograph what you have

Document upload

Phone photos, PDFs, Word documents — any quality. The system reads them, classifies them across 57 document types, extracts key fields, and places them on the timeline. Spanish and English.

03 — See what you hold

The clear view

Your case file shows what's present, pending, and critically missing. The gap map. The reconstructed timeline. The questions to bring to your lawyer. The organized picture.

The founding case

Built in a real estate.
A contested one.

"My father was Venezuelan. He had three apartments in Florida, bank accounts in Miami, and two sons in California. I was in Argentina when he died. Nobody handed me a guide for that."

AcervoVista was built during a real multi-jurisdictional estate dispute — Venezuela, Florida, and Argentina. Four heirs. Adversarial factions. Missing documents. Suspicious pre-death transfers. A decedent who used legal structures to obscure assets.

A practicing attorney reviewed the first version and was impressed — not by legal analysis, but by the organization, the timeline, and the gaps it surfaced. That reaction is the proof of concept.
